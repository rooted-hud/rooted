# Importance of Nutrition on Health in America

## Health starts with what's on your plate.

Healthy bodies and minds require nutritious meals at every age. However, millions of people across the nation
face deep-rooted barriers to accessing healthy food, including high grocery prices. These barriers increase
the risk of chronic conditions like diabetes, heart disease and high blood pressure—making our communities
less healthy, productive and resilient.

![Josephine at a 'food pharmacy' in Houston](/sites/default/files/2026-01/Adriana%20400X375.jpg)

In our [2025 Elevating Voices: Insights Report](https://www.feedingamerica.org/research/community-solutions-hunger), our
neighbors facing hunger further emphasize the connection between hunger, health and affordability.

In the report, many neighbors say that what they can afford to put on their plates is often not food that
nourishes their physical and mental well-being. Neighbors describe being priced out of purchasing nutritious
foods, opting for less expensive but less healthy alternatives.

* 68% of surveyed neighbors expressed wanting to eat healthier, but not being able to afford to right now.
* In the past year, 66% of surveyed neighbors skipped meals or ate less food due to high food prices.
* In the past year, 80% of surveyed neighbors bought cheaper, less nutritious food due to high food
  prices.

## Feeding America is helping build the foundation for good health.

![Jackson, 6, eats a summer meal at the Ken Rock Community Center in Rockford, Illinois.](/sites/default/files/2025-10/Jackson%20300X300.jpg)

We are working to remove hurdles to better nutrition that supports better health.

* Neighbors can receive dietitian-designed meals with culturally relevant foods that match their tastes
  and medical needs at their local food banks.
* Over 50 registered dietitians and more than 200 dedicated nutrition staff across the Feeding America
  network provide nutrition education and support navigating the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program
  (SNAP).
* The Feeding America network has helped over 145,000 neighbors access healthy food prescriptions through
  community-based partnerships.

By meeting our neighbors where they are—at food banks, health clinics or mobile distributions—we are building
solutions that are rooted in dignity, accessibility and impact.

With support from the Elevance Health Foundation, Feeding America is advancing a "Food as Medicine"
approach that strengthens connections between food banks and healthcare providers. Neighbors facing
hunger can access dietitian-designed meals with culturally relevant foods that match their tastes
and medical needs, including healthy food prescriptions for diet-related illnesses, right at their
local food banks. To date, over 145,000 neighbors have accessed healthy food prescriptions through
this community-based program.

[Learn more](https://www.feedingamerica.org/partners/why-i-partner/elevance-health-foundation#main-content)
about Elevance Health Foundation's support of Feeding America.
