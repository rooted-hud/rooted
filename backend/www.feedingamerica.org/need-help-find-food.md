# Find Free Food and Groceries

## If you need food today

Your local food bank can help you find food today, even if you need temporary help. They partner with food pantries, soup kitchens, and meal programs in your local community to give away free food.

Enter your zip code to find the food bank partnering with Feeding America. They'll share the locations and hours of nearby food distributions.

**Need additional assistance finding food?**

Call Hunger Free America's Hunger Hotline for additional support with finding food now. When you call, staff will connect you to food providers and other resources in your community.  
  
The hotline is available every Monday through Friday from 8 a.m to 8 p.m. ET. Call 1-866-348-6479 for English operators and 1-877-842-6273 for Spanish operators.

## Federal programs that can help

Many federal programs are available whether you are here for the first time or have used food assistance.

### SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program)

* [SNAP](https://www.feedingamerica.org/our-work/hunger-relief-programs/snap?s_src=W21BREFER&s_referrer=https%253A%252F%252Fwww.feedingamerica.org%252F&s_subsrc=https%253A%252F%252Fsecure.feedingamerica.org%252Fsite%252FDonation2%253Fdf_id%253D29950%252629950.donation%253Dform1&s_src=W21BREFER&s_referrer=https%253A%252F%252Fwww.feedingamerica.org%252F&s_subsrc=https%253A%252F%252Fsecure.feedingamerica.org%252Fsite%252FDonation2%253Fdf_id%253D29950%252629950.donation%253Dform1) (formerly known as food stamps) currently provides 1 in 7 Americans with help making ends meet. [Find out if you are eligible](https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/recipient/eligibility) and learn how to access your SNAP benefits. In addition, we can [connect you with your nearest food bank's application assistance program](https://www.feedingamerica.org/need-help-find-food/apply-for-food-assistance?s_src=W21BREFER&s_referrer=https%253A%252F%252Fwww.feedingamerica.org%252F&s_subsrc=https%253A%252F%252Fsecure.feedingamerica.org%252Fsite%252FDonation2%253Fdf_id%253D29950%252629950.donation%253Dform1&s_src=W21BREFER&s_referrer=https%253A%252F%252Fwww.feedingamerica.org%252F&s_subsrc=https%253A%252F%252Fsecure.feedingamerica.org%252Fsite%252FDonation2%253Fdf_id%253D29950%252629950.donation%253Dform1).

  **Important SNAP update:**The recent government shutdown and the "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" may impact your SNAP benefits. [Learn more](https://www.feedingamerica.org/our-work/hunger-relief-programs/snap#shutdown).

### WIC (Women, Infant & Children) Programs

* [WIC (Women, Infant and Children) programs](https://www.feedingamerica.org/need-help-find-food/wic) provide healthy, supplemental food to low-income people who are pregnant and have children ages five and under. [Find out if you're eligible for WIC](https://www.fns.usda.gov/wic/wic-eligibility-requirements)  and contact your local WIC office to apply.

  **Important WIC update:** The recent government shutdown may impact your WIC benefits. November 2025 WIC benefits are expected to be paid out in full, but they may be delayed depending on your state’s processes. WIC benefits in December and January are expected to be on time and in full. [Your local WIC office](https://www.fns.usda.gov/wic/program-contacts) can provide more information on how benefits may be impacted in your state.

## Programs for your situation

### Help for families with a child or teen

* **Grocery Programs for Kids and Families**

  [Contact your local food bank](https://www.feedingamerica.org/find-your-local-foodbank) for more information on special [programs to feed kids](https://www.feedingamerica.org/our-work/hunger-relief-programs/school-year-meals-programs). Programs include the [School Pantry Program](https://www.feedingamerica.org/our-work/hunger-relief-programs/school-pantry "School Pantry Program"), where parents can conveniently pick up food at their child’s school, and the [BackPack Program](https://www.feedingamerica.org/our-work/hunger-relief-programs/backpack-program "BackPack Program"), which gives children food to take home for the weekends.

### Help for families with babies or young kids

* **Help with Baby Food, Formula, or Diapers**

  Many food banks help with free baby food, formula, and diapers. They can also connect you to your local diaper bank. [Ask your local food bank](https://www.feedingamerica.org/find-your-local-foodbank) about free diapers and free baby formula.

  **WIC (Women and Infant Children) Programs**

  [WIC](https://www.feedingamerica.org/need-help-find-food/wic) helps low-income people who are pregnant and have young children with food assistance and health care referrals. [Find out if you're eligible](https://www.fns.usda.gov/wic/wic-eligibility-requirements) and [contact your state WIC agency to apply](https://www.fns.usda.gov/wic/state-agency).

### Help for people over 60

* **Senior Programs**

  Many food banks host [senior food programs](https://www.feedingamerica.org/our-work/hunger-relief-programs/senior-programs) that provide free groceries to older adults, with no strings attached. [Contact your local food bank](https://www.feedingamerica.org/find-your-local-foodbank) to find out if a senior program is available near you.

  **Comprehensive help for seniors in your area**

  [BenefitsCheckUp.org](https://www.benefitscheckup.org/) is a free online service of the [National Council on Aging](https://www.ncoa.org/). Just answer a few simple questions and you will receive a personalized report listing the programs near you that you may be eligible for, program details, and how to apply in your area. This includes information about programs that can help you pay for medications, health care, utilities, and more.

### Help for people who have experienced a disaster

* When a natural disaster strikes, we know it’s a huge struggle to recover and rebuild your life. Thankfully, there are many organizations you can turn to for help.

  + [The Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA)](https://www.fema.gov/individual-disaster-assistance): works in areas hardest hit by natural disasters to help communities recover – and offers many services to help families get their lives back to normal
  + [The Red Cross](https://www.redcross.org/get-help.html): on the ground following many natural disasters. They provide relief and resources to help communities experiencing crisis
  + [Feeding America network of food banks and meal programs](https://www.feedingamerica.org/find-your-local-foodbank): built to provide nationwide relief and recovery support to affected communities

## Other programs that help make ends meet

### Help with finding work and job training

* Looking for a job or a way to build new skills? Explore local work and job training opportunities! Whether you're looking to start a new career, improve your skills, or get the training you need, there are resources available to help you succeed. [Find a program or opportunity near you.](https://www.careeronestop.org/LocalHelp/local-help.aspx)

### Help with tax credits and tax filing

* **Tax Benefits**

  The Child Tax Credit and the Earned Income Tax Credit may help you get a tax break. These credits can provide extra money for groceries, gas, childcare, and more. Find out if you’re eligible for the Child Tax Credit or eligible for the Earned Income Tax Credit.

  **Free online filing:**Use GetCTC to claim your Child Tax Credit and your third stimulus payment. Filing takes 10-15 minutes and does not require any tax documents.

  **Free Tax Assistance**

  If you need help filing your taxes, free tax preparation support is available from these trusted sources:

  + IRS: Visit the [VITA Site Locator](https://irs.treasury.gov/freetaxprep/) to find in-person options for free tax support.
  + [IRS Free File](https://www.irs.gov/filing/free-file-do-your-federal-taxes-for-free) offers a free online way to prepare and file federal taxes.
  + [United Way](https://myfreetaxes.com) offers free self-file and online and in-person options for tax assistance.
  + [Direct File](https://www.irs.gov/filing/irs-direct-file-for-free) is available in 25 states and provides a simple, secure, and free way to file your taxes online directly with the IRS.

### Help with health insurance

* Get information on finding and signing up for health insurance at [Healthcare.Gov](https://www.healthcare.gov/). You may qualify for [Medicaid or the Children's Health Insurance Program](https://www.healthcare.gov/lower-costs/). You can also get [application help](https://localhelp.healthcare.gov/#/).

### Help with heat, electricity, utility and internet bills

* The [Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program](https://www.acf.hhs.gov/ocs/low-income-home-energy-assistance-program-liheap) helps families lower their energy costs. Find out more about LIHEAP by calling 1-866-674-6327 or visiting the [LIHEAP Clearinghouse](https://liheapch.acf.hhs.gov/search-tool/).

  The [Low Income Household Water Assistance Program](https://www.acf.hhs.gov/ocs/programs/lihwap) provides grants to help families pay their water and wastewater bills. Find out more about LIHWAP by contacting [your local program](https://www.acf.hhs.gov/ocs/map/lihwap-map-state-and-territory-contact-listing) or your [tribal contact](https://www.acf.hhs.gov/ocs/map/lihwap-map-tribal-contact-listing).

### Help for other family needs

* **Dial 2-1-1**

  For help with things like utilities, housing, mental healthcare, or childcare, dial 211 on any phone. Speak with a trained professional in your area 24 hours a day. Services are free and confidential and available in many languages. While most of the United States is covered by 211, the service is not available in all areas.
